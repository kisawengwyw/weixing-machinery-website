Hydraulic Hose Fittings

Hydraulic hose fittings are used to connect flexible hydraulic hoses to pumps, valves, cylinders, manifolds and other fluid power components. They include crimp fittings, ferrules, swivel nuts, hose tails, elbows and adapters designed for secure hose assembly and reliable oil flow. Good hose fittings must match the hose inner diameter, pressure rating, thread standard and bending direction. They are widely used in mobile machinery, industrial equipment, agricultural machines and maintenance replacement markets.

Selection Advice

Hydraulic Hose Fittings

Choose hydraulic hose fittings according to hose size, hose construction, working pressure and end connection standard.

For crimp fittings, always match the ferrule and insert to the specific hose type to avoid leakage or hose pull-off.

Use elbow fittings when hose routing requires direction change and when bending radius must be controlled.

Do not mix thread standards without verification, especially BSP, JIC, ORFS, NPT and metric connections.

Select stainless steel or enhanced plating for outdoor machinery, salt spray exposure or corrosive environments.

Provide hose samples or assembly drawings for custom hose fitting production to ensure correct crimping dimensions.

Technical Guide

Common Hydraulic Hose Fitting Types

Common hydraulic hose fitting types include straight hose fittings for direct hose routing, 45-degree hose fittings and 90-degree hose fittings for controlled direction changes, female swivel fittings for easier alignment, and male threaded hose fittings for connection to ports, hydraulic hose adapters and equipment interfaces.

Hose tail fittings, crimp fittings, reusable fittings and custom hose end fittings are selected according to the hose construction and assembly method. Different structures are used for hydraulic hose assemblies in mobile machinery, construction equipment, agricultural machinery and maintenance replacement, where routing space, vibration and service access can all affect fitting choice.

Technical Guide

Hose End Connection and Sealing Options

Hydraulic hose fittings connect flexible hydraulic hoses to pumps, valves, cylinders, manifolds and pipe systems. The hose end connection must suit the port or mating adapter so the assembly can be installed without thread damage, hose twist or sealing misalignment.

Common connection options include JIC, BSP, NPT, ORFS and metric thread forms where suitable. Correct thread type, sealing method and hose compatibility are important for a safe and reliable hydraulic hose assembly, especially when hydraulic hose end fittings are used in compact equipment with repeated pressure cycles.

Technical Guide

Crimp Ferrule and Hose Tail Structure

The hose tail, barbed insert or nipple section supports the inner side of the hose and helps create mechanical retention after assembly. The crimp ferrule or sleeve compresses the hose reinforcement around the fitting tail, forming the grip needed for crimp hose fittings.

The hose tail must match the hose inner diameter, while the crimp sleeve must match the hose outer diameter and hose type. Incorrect matching may cause leakage, hose pull-off or reduced service life, so ferrule selection should be checked together with the hose brand, construction and target crimp diameter.

Technical Guide

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Common material choices for hydraulic hose fittings include carbon steel and stainless steel, depending on the working environment and hydraulic system requirements. Carbon steel is often used for general machinery applications, while stainless steel may be selected for more corrosive or washdown environments.

Surface finish options may include trivalent zinc plating, zinc nickel plating, black oxide or stainless polished finish where suitable. Material and surface finish should be selected based on corrosion resistance, pressure requirement, hose assembly environment and equipment application.

Buyer Guide

How to Choose Hydraulic Hose Fittings

When choosing hydraulic hose fittings, buyers should check hose size, hose type, working pressure, thread standard, sealing method, fitting angle, ferrule type, material, surface finish and installation space. These details help confirm whether the fitting can be assembled correctly and routed safely in the machine.

Also confirm whether the application requires straight, elbow, female swivel, male threaded, crimp or reusable hose fitting forms. For replacement projects, photos, old samples or assembly drawings can help identify the correct hydraulic hose adapters and avoid mixing similar-looking thread standards.
