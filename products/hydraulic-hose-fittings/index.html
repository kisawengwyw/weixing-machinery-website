Hydraulic Hose Fittings

Product Summary

Hydraulic hose fittings form part of a complete hose assembly and must be selected as a compatible system with the hose construction, insert or stem, ferrule, crimp specification and equipment-side connection. Product configurations may include hose inserts or stems, crimp ferrules, swivel nuts, straight, 45° and 90° hose ends, one-piece or two-piece designs and equipment-side connection ends.

Hydraulic hose fittings are used to connect flexible hydraulic hoses to pumps, valves, cylinders, manifolds and other fluid power components. The hose-side insert or stem, ferrule, hose construction, insertion depth and approved crimp data work together for retention and sealing, while the equipment-side seal is determined separately by the selected JIC, ORFS, BSP, NPT, metric or custom interface.

Selection Advice

How to Choose Hydraulic Hose Fittings

1. Identify the hose. Confirm the exact hose type, nominal size and construction.

2. Confirm hose construction details. Check reinforcement type, hose outside diameter and skive/no-skive requirement.

3. Select compatible parts. Choose a compatible insert or stem and ferrule series.

4. Confirm crimp data. Verify the approved crimp diameter, insertion depth and assembly method.

5. Confirm equipment-side sealing. Check the equipment-side interface and sealing method.

6. Select orientation. Choose straight, 45° or 90° orientation and swivel requirements.

7. Confirm assembly dimensions. Review overall assembly length and angular orientation.

8. Confirm operating conditions. Check material, finish, media, temperature and system requirements.

9. Confirm complete assembly requirements. The working requirement applies to the complete hose assembly, not the fitting alone.

10. Send drawings and hose data. Provide drawings, hose data and samples for custom production.

Technical Guide

Technical Guide

Hydraulic hose fittings must be selected with the hose construction, reinforcement type, hose outside diameter, skive/no-skive requirement, insert or stem, ferrule, insertion depth and approved crimp diameter. Nominal hose size alone is not sufficient for fitting selection.

Do not combine a hose, insert, ferrule and crimp diameter from unrelated systems unless the complete combination has been technically reviewed and validated. Non-standard combinations require drawing, assembly and validation review.

Confirm insertion depth, skive or no-skive requirement, final crimp diameter, assembly length and elbow orientation. After assembly, inspect hose twist, insert position and ferrule condition. All specific values must follow approved hose-and-fitting data or drawings.

One-piece fittings have the ferrule preassembled or combined with the fitting according to the product series. Two-piece fittings use separately selected inserts or stems and ferrules. Neither structure is automatically safer or higher-pressure; selection depends on hose series, assembly equipment, service method and approved crimp data.

Pressure, proof, burst or impulse testing can be arranged only when specified by the order, drawing or validation plan.

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Carbon steel and stainless steel are the normal material choices; other materials are subject to hose system, media and application review. Brass can be evaluated for selected low-pressure, media-specific or project requirements.

Surface finish options include trivalent zinc plating, zinc-nickel coating, black oxide, phosphating and natural stainless finish, subject to material and order requirements. Selection should consider corrosion exposure, assembly environment and customer specification.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hydraulic hose fitting?
A hydraulic hose fitting is part of a complete hose assembly and connects the hose to equipment-side ports or adapters.

Is crimp/ferrule a thread type?
No. Crimping and ferrules are hose-side assembly structures, not thread types. Equipment-side connections such as JIC, ORFS, BSP, NPT or metric define the mating interface.

What components must be compatible?
The hose construction, insert or stem, ferrule, approved crimp data and equipment-side connection must be compatible as a system.

How does the hose side seal and retain the hose?
The hose insert or stem, hose, ferrule, insertion depth and approved crimp diameter work together to retain and seal the hose side.

How does the equipment-side connection seal?
The equipment-side end seals according to its specific interface, such as JIC flare, ORFS face seal, BSP design, NPT taper thread or metric connection.

How is working pressure confirmed?
Working pressure is confirmed from the compatible hose, insert or stem, ferrule, equipment-side connection and approved crimp data. The complete assembly is limited by its lowest-rated component and validated assembly requirements.

Can fittings from different hose systems be mixed?
Do not mix hose, insert, ferrule and crimp data from unrelated systems unless the complete combination has been technically reviewed and validated.

What is the difference between one-piece and two-piece fittings?
One-piece fittings have the ferrule preassembled or combined with the fitting according to the product series. Two-piece fittings use separately selected inserts or stems and ferrules.

Can crimp hose fittings be reused?
Crimp fittings should not be assumed to be reusable. Reuse is permitted only when the specific product design, hose system and approved assembly procedure explicitly allow it.

What materials and finishes are available?
Materials include carbon steel and stainless steel, with other materials reviewed by application. Finishes include trivalent zinc plating, zinc-nickel coating, black oxide, phosphating or natural stainless finish.

What information is needed for quotation?
Provide hose type, nominal size, construction, outside diameter, skive requirement, insert or stem series, ferrule series, crimp data, equipment-side connection, assembly length, orientation, material, finish, quantity and drawings or samples.

Can custom hose fittings be manufactured by drawing or sample?
Yes. Wei Xing Machinery can manufacture custom hose fittings according to drawings, hose data, samples and approved assembly requirements.
