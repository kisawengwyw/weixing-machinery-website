Hydraulic Manifold Blocks

Product Summary

Hydraulic manifold blocks integrate drilled, milled and tapped oil passages into one compact block, reducing external pipework and potential leakage points. They are manufactured to customer drawings for hydraulic power units, valve groups and compact equipment circuits.

Hydraulic manifold blocks are precision-machined metal blocks used to distribute, control and connect hydraulic oil flow inside compact hydraulic systems. They integrate multiple ports, threaded cavities, valve mounting surfaces and internal flow passages into one component, reducing external piping and improving system organization. Manifold blocks can be manufactured from aluminum, carbon steel or stainless steel according to pressure and application needs. They are used in power units, cartridge valve systems, mobile machinery and industrial hydraulic equipment.

Technical Guide

Hydraulic Manifold Blocks Technical Guide

Manifold blocks depend on accurate relationships between ports, cross-drilled passages and plug sealing positions. The machining plan should define datums for each face so intersecting passages meet correctly without leaving excessive burrs or weak wall thickness.

Circuit reliability is strongly affected by deburring and cleaning inside blind holes, cross holes and threaded ports. Drawings should identify port standards, plug types, cavity depths, pressure test requirements and any faces that must remain flat for valve mounting.

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Carbon steel is suitable for strength and cost-sensitive hydraulic circuits, while aluminum alloy is often selected for lighter manifold blocks and easier machining. Stainless steel can be used when corrosion resistance or fluid compatibility is more important than material cost.

Black oxide, anodizing, zinc plating and anti-rust oil are common finish choices. Finish selection should consider port threads, sealing faces, plug areas and whether coating thickness may affect valve mounting or thread gauge fit.
