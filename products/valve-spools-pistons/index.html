Valve Components

Valve Spools & Pistons

Valve spools and pistons are precision cylindrical components used in hydraulic valves, control valves, cylinders and servo-related mechanisms. They control fluid direction, pressure, flow or mechanical movement by sliding inside matched bores, sleeves or cylinder bodies. These parts require accurate diameter tolerance, smooth surface finish, groove geometry and wear-resistant material. Valve spools and pistons are commonly produced by CNC turning, grinding, polishing and heat treatment for stable hydraulic control and long service life.

Selection Advice

Valve Spools & Pistons

  • Choose valve spools and pistons according to bore size, fit clearance, pressure condition and required movement accuracy.
  • For precision valve spools, provide sleeve samples or clearance requirements so the sliding fit can be controlled correctly.
  • Use heat treatment, hard chrome or nitriding when the part must resist repeated sliding wear.
  • Do not ignore burr control around holes, windows and grooves because small burrs may cause valve sticking.
  • For replacement parts, compare every groove width, hole position and end shape with the original sample.
  • For batch production, define diameter tolerance, roughness and hardness clearly before manufacturing starts.
Technical Guide

Common Valve Spool and Piston Types

Common valve spools and pistons include hydraulic valve spools, control valve spools, servo valve spools, directional valve spools, hydraulic pistons, stepped pistons, grooved pistons and custom CNC-machined spool and piston components. Different structures are used in hydraulic valves, cylinders, control systems, construction machinery, industrial equipment and OEM hydraulic assemblies depending on the motion, sealing and flow-control requirements.

Technical Guide

Spool Geometry and Flow Control Features

Hydraulic valve spools often include stepped diameters, ring grooves, metering edges, radial holes, long slots, windows and precision sliding surfaces. These geometry details affect oil flow direction, flow rate, pressure control and valve response, so the spool must be machined with the intended hydraulic function in mind.

Dimensional accuracy and surface finish are important because the spool usually works with a valve body bore or sleeve. Controlled diameters, clean edges and smooth sliding areas help CNC machined hydraulic components fit correctly and move consistently in the matched assembly.

Technical Guide

Piston Sealing Grooves and Matching Requirements

Hydraulic pistons may include seal grooves, guide ring grooves, threaded sections, shoulders, oil holes and precision outer diameters. Groove size, sealing surface quality, concentricity and matching clearance are important for stable cylinder or valve performance.

Incorrect groove dimensions or rough surfaces may affect sealing, movement and service life. For custom hydraulic pistons, the groove width, groove depth, edge radius and outer diameter should be checked against the seal design and the matching cylinder or valve bore.

Technical Guide

CNC Machining and Surface Quality Requirements

Valve spools and pistons usually require precision CNC turning, milling, drilling, grooving, grinding, deburring and polishing where required. Diameter tolerance, roundness, straightness, edge quality, surface roughness and burr control all influence how the parts assemble and slide.

Stable machining control helps the parts fit correctly with valve bodies, sleeves, seals and hydraulic cylinder assemblies. For custom valve spool manufacturing, careful control around grooves, holes, slots and metering edges is especially important because small machining variations can change fit or hydraulic response.

Technical Guide

Materials and Surface Treatment Options

Common material choices for valve spools and pistons include alloy steel, carbon steel, stainless steel and other customer-specified metals depending on pressure, wear resistance, corrosion resistance and working environment. The selected material should match the part's load, sliding condition and fluid exposure.

Surface treatment options may include polishing, black oxide, hard chrome plating, nitriding, zinc plating or other specified finishes where suitable. Material and surface treatment should be selected based on wear resistance, corrosion resistance, sliding performance and the application environment.

Buyer Guide

How to Choose Valve Spools and Pistons

When selecting valve spools and pistons, buyers should check drawing dimensions, material grade, outer diameter tolerance, groove dimensions, hole position, surface roughness, hardness requirement, sealing method, matching valve body or cylinder bore and application environment. These details help confirm whether the component is suitable for the required pressure, movement and assembly condition.

For custom valve spool manufacturing or custom hydraulic pistons, provide drawings, samples or assembly requirements whenever possible. Clear information about the mating bore, sleeve, seal type and working environment helps avoid mismatch during production and final assembly.
