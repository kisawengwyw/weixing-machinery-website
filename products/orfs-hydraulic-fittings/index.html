ORFS Hydraulic Fittings

Product Summary

ORFS hydraulic fittings are O-ring face seal connectors for high-pressure hydraulic systems. ORFS connection geometry can be manufactured according to applicable SAE J1453 and ISO 8434-3 requirements when specified by the drawing and order, and the fitting seals by compressing an O-ring on the flat mating face rather than relying on thread sealing. Wei Xing Machinery supplies custom CNC-machined ORFS fittings, adapters and connection parts with drawing-based options for size, thread, material and surface treatment.

ORFS hydraulic fittings are designed for high-pressure hydraulic systems that require clean, stable and leak-resistant connections. The O-ring face seal structure places the sealing element on the flat face of the fitting, helping reduce leakage caused by vibration, pressure fluctuation and repeated assembly. These fittings are widely used in mobile machinery, hydraulic power units, oil cylinders and industrial fluid power pipelines where reliable sealing performance and long service life are important.

Selection Advice

How to Choose ORFS Hydraulic Fittings

1. Confirm connection size. Check the specified ORFS connection size, unified straight thread and mating port before ordering.

2. Confirm sealing face and O-ring material. Verify the flat mating face condition and select NBR or FKM according to the fluid, temperature and application requirements.

3. Choose fitting type. Select straight, elbow, tee, bulkhead or swivel forms according to the hydraulic line layout.

4. Choose body material. Match carbon steel or stainless steel to the working environment. Stainless steel grades are confirmed according to the drawing and order requirements.

5. Confirm surface treatment. Select trivalent zinc plating, zinc-nickel coating, black oxide or natural stainless finish according to material and order requirements.

6. Send drawing or sample for custom production. Provide drawings, samples or assembly requirements when non-standard dimensions are needed.

Choose ORFS fittings when the hydraulic system requires stronger leakage control than ordinary tapered or metal-to-metal threaded connections.

For equipment exposed to vibration, pressure shock or frequent maintenance, the face seal structure can provide a more stable sealing solution.

Select carbon steel with trivalent zinc plating for general industrial use, and stainless steel for corrosive or outdoor environments.

Always confirm connection size, tube size, O-ring material, mating component, material and system requirements before placing an order.

When replacing older fittings, compare the sealing face structure carefully to avoid mixing ORFS with JIC, BSP or metric thread types.

For custom hydraulic equipment, provide drawings or samples so that dimensions, tolerances and surface finish can be matched accurately.

Common ORFS hydraulic fittings include straight adapters for in-line hose or tube runs, 90-degree elbows for directional changes in compact equipment, tee fittings for branch circuits, bulkhead unions for passing through panels or brackets, and female swivel fittings for easier assembly in service locations.

For OEM hydraulic equipment and maintenance replacement, custom CNC-machined ORFS adapters can be produced to match drawing dimensions, connection combinations and installation space. Different structures are selected according to hydraulic pipeline routing, tube connection requirements, compact equipment layouts and replacement needs during field maintenance.

Technical Guide

ORFS Face Seal Structure

ORFS fittings seal by compressing an O-ring against the flat mating face. The straight thread provides assembly force but is not the primary sealing location. Confirm the connection size, mating face, O-ring material, fitting configuration and system requirements before ordering.

Do not rely on pipe-thread sealant as the primary seal at the ORFS face-seal location. ORFS connection geometry can be manufactured according to applicable SAE J1453 and ISO 8434-3 requirements when specified by the drawing and order.

Technical Guide

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Material selection for ORFS hydraulic fittings should match the working environment and the fluid system requirements. Carbon steel is often used for general hydraulic machinery, while stainless steel can be considered where corrosion resistance or outdoor exposure is a higher priority.

Surface treatment options include trivalent zinc plating, zinc-nickel coating, black oxide or natural stainless finish, subject to material and order requirements. Other materials can be evaluated according to drawing, media and application requirements.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What are ORFS hydraulic fittings?
ORFS hydraulic fittings are O-ring face seal connectors used to join hoses, tubes, ports and adapters in hydraulic systems.

How do ORFS fittings seal?
They seal by compressing an O-ring in a machined face groove between two flat sealing faces, rather than using the thread as the main seal.

What standards are used?
Applicable ORFS connection dimensions can be manufactured according to SAE J1453 and ISO 8434-3 when specified by the drawing and order.

What is the difference between ORFS and JIC fittings?
ORFS fittings use an O-ring face seal, while JIC fittings use a 37° metal flare seal. ORFS is often selected for stronger leakage resistance in high-pressure or vibration-prone systems.

What materials are available?
Materials include carbon steel and stainless steel; other materials can be evaluated according to drawing, media and application requirements. Stainless steel grades are confirmed according to the drawing and order requirements.

What surface treatments are available?
Trivalent zinc plating, zinc-nickel coating, black oxide or natural stainless finish can be selected subject to material and order requirements.

How is working pressure selected?
Pressure requirement is confirmed according to fitting size, material, configuration, mating component and system requirements.

Can you manufacture ORFS fittings according to drawings?
Yes. Custom CNC manufacturing can be arranged according to drawings, samples, connection combinations, material requirements and surface treatment needs.

What industries use ORFS hydraulic fittings?
Typical industries include construction machinery, agricultural equipment, mining equipment, industrial hydraulic systems, marine equipment and oil & gas equipment.
