Custom CNC Parts

Product Summary

Custom CNC parts are made-to-drawing hydraulic and industrial components produced according to customer material, tolerance, surface finish and inspection requirements. They are suitable for non-standard OEM parts, replacement components and prototypes that standard fittings cannot cover.

Custom CNC parts are precision-machined metal components manufactured according to drawings, samples or functional requirements. They can include hydraulic fittings, valve components, shafts, bushings, pistons, threaded connectors, brackets and special-shaped parts. CNC turning, milling, drilling, tapping, grinding and surface treatment allow the parts to meet dimensional, functional and appearance requirements. Custom CNC parts are suitable for hydraulic equipment, machinery manufacturing, automotive components, industrial automation and replacement projects that require accurate and repeatable production.

Selection Advice

Custom CNC Parts Selection Advice

1. Confirm drawing requirements. Provide key dimensions, tolerances, threads, holes, grooves and surface roughness.

2. Confirm material. Select material according to pressure, weldability, corrosion resistance and cost.

3. Confirm surface finish. Specify salt-spray, color, coating or anti-rust packaging requirements.

4. Confirm inspection focus. Mark sealing faces, fitted bores, threads and critical dimensions.

Technical Guide

Custom CNC Parts Technical Guide

Custom CNC parts should be reviewed from the drawing function first, not only from the outside shape. Datums, tolerance chains, critical features and inspection methods need to be confirmed before machining so prototype and batch parts remain consistent.

Turning, milling, drilling, tapping and grinding can be combined for one part when the drawing includes tight fits, threaded ports, grooves or sealing faces. Clear revision control helps prevent mixing old and new dimensions during repeat orders.

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Materials are selected according to the customer drawing, mechanical load, corrosion exposure, tolerance requirement and cost target. Carbon steel, stainless steel, alloy steel, aluminum, brass and engineering plastics can all be considered when suitable.

Surface treatment should be matched to function and batch consistency, including zinc plating, black oxide, anodizing, passivation, polishing, phosphating or painting. Critical tolerances and fitted areas should be reviewed before coating to avoid dimensional drift.
