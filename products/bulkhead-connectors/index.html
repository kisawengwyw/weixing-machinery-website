Bulkhead Connectors

Product Summary

Bulkhead connectors provide a fixed hydraulic pass-through point on panels, frames and brackets. The extended threaded body, shoulder and locknut arrangement help stabilize the connection while the end styles can be matched to BSP, JIC, ORFS, NPT, metric or custom interfaces. Custom machining can adjust panel thickness, thread combination and locknut style.

Bulkhead connectors are hydraulic and pneumatic style fittings used to pass a fluid line through panels, brackets, machine frames or equipment walls. The connector body is usually fixed with lock nuts on one or both sides of the panel, creating a stable connection point for tubes, hoses or adapters. Bulkhead connectors are available in straight, elbow and reducing configurations and are useful when a pipeline must be routed cleanly through a mounting plate while maintaining serviceability.

Selection Advice

How to Choose Bulkhead Connectors

1. Confirm thread size. Check the exact thread size and mating component before ordering.

2. Confirm sealing method. Verify whether sealing uses cone, flare, O-ring, washer, taper thread, sealant or hose crimp structure.

3. Choose fitting type. Select straight, elbow, tee, swivel, bulkhead, hose fitting or transition adapter according to the line layout.

4. Choose material. Match carbon steel, stainless steel or brass to pressure, corrosion and media requirements.

5. Confirm surface treatment. Select trivalent zinc, zinc nickel, black oxide, phosphating or stainless finish for the working environment.

6. Send drawing or sample for custom production. Provide drawings, samples or assembly requirements when non-standard dimensions are needed.

Technical Guide

Technical Guide

Bulkhead connectors combine a hydraulic connection end with a mounting shoulder and locknut. Panel thickness, hole diameter and wrench clearance must be confirmed so the connector clamps securely.

The sealing method at each end follows the selected thread family, such as JIC flare, BSP 60° cone/washer, NPT taper or ORFS O-ring face seal.

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Carbon steel is commonly selected for general hydraulic machinery, stainless steel for corrosion resistance, and brass for selected lower-pressure or media-specific applications.

Surface finish options include trivalent zinc, zinc nickel, black oxide, phosphating and natural stainless finish. Selection should consider corrosion exposure, assembly environment and customer specification.
