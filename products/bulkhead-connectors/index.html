Hydraulic Fittings

Bulkhead Connectors

Bulkhead connectors are hydraulic and pneumatic style fittings used to pass a fluid line through panels, brackets, machine frames or equipment walls. The connector body is usually fixed with lock nuts on one or both sides of the panel, creating a stable connection point for tubes, hoses or adapters. Bulkhead connectors are available in straight, elbow and reducing configurations and are useful when a pipeline must be routed cleanly through a mounting plate while maintaining serviceability.

Selection Advice

Bulkhead Connectors

Choose bulkhead connectors when a hydraulic line must pass through a panel while staying fixed, clean and easy to service.

Confirm panel thickness before ordering because body length and lock nut engagement must match the mounting plate.

Select the correct thread standard on both sides to match hoses, tubes or adapters already used in the system.

For vibrating mobile equipment, use proper locking and support to prevent loosening around the panel.

Use stainless steel bulkhead connectors for corrosive, marine or outdoor applications with long exposure time.

Provide panel drawings for custom products so thread length, nut position and clearance can be designed accurately.

Technical Guide

Common Bulkhead Connector Types

Common bulkhead connector types include straight bulkhead unions, bulkhead elbows, bulkhead tee connectors, male bulkhead adapters, female bulkhead adapters, ORFS bulkhead fittings, JIC bulkhead fittings, BSP bulkhead adapters, NPT bulkhead connectors and custom bulkhead fittings.

Different structures are selected for different installation needs, such as hydraulic panels, equipment frames, hose routing, tube connections and maintenance replacement. For example, straight bulkhead connectors are useful for direct panel pass-through points, while elbows and tee styles help route hydraulic lines where space or direction changes are limited.

Technical Guide

Panel Mounting and Lock Nut Structure

Panel mount hydraulic connectors are designed to pass through a panel, bracket, frame or partition and are usually fixed by one or two lock nuts. The extended threaded body allows the connector to be secured at a defined mounting point instead of leaving the hose or tube unsupported.

Thread length, lock nut position, panel thickness, sealing surface and wrench clearance are important for correct installation. A stable bulkhead connection helps keep hydraulic lines organized and reduces movement at the panel connection point, especially where equipment vibration or service access must be considered.

Technical Guide

Thread and Sealing Options

Hydraulic bulkhead fittings may use different connection standards such as JIC, BSP, NPT, ORFS or metric threads depending on the hydraulic system and the matching hose, tube or adapter connection.

Suitable sealing options may include flare sealing, O-ring face sealing, tapered thread sealing, bonded seal sealing or metal-to-metal cone sealing. Correct thread identification and sealing form are important to avoid leakage, thread damage or mismatched installation during assembly and replacement.

Technical Guide

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Common material choices for bulkhead adapters include carbon steel, stainless steel and brass, selected according to the working environment, pressure requirement and corrosion resistance needs of the equipment.

Surface finish options may include trivalent zinc plating, zinc nickel plating, black oxide, polishing or a stainless steel finish where suitable. Material and surface finish should be selected based on hydraulic pressure, corrosion resistance, panel environment and the final equipment application.

Selection Guide

How to Choose Bulkhead Connectors

When choosing bulkhead connectors, check the thread standard, connection type, panel thickness, lock nut size, pressure rating, sealing method, material, surface finish and available installation space. The selected connector should also match the hose fitting, tube fitting or adapter used on each side of the panel.

Confirm whether the required connector is straight, elbow, tee, male, female, ORFS, JIC, BSP, NPT or a custom type. Clear drawings or samples help define the mounting length, wrench flats, sealing face and nut position before production.
