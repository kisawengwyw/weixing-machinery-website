Welded Tube Assemblies

Product Summary

Custom welded tube assemblies combine bent or straight tubes, machined adapters, bosses, brackets and flanges into fixed hydraulic routing components for OEM equipment. They reduce loose threaded joints, improve repeatable installation and support compact hydraulic layouts where flexible hose is not preferred.

Welded tube assemblies are customized hydraulic pipe and structural connection components made by combining tubes, adapters, fittings, bosses, brackets or flanges through welding and machining. They are used when standard adapters cannot meet the required routing, strength, shape or installation position. Proper welded tube assemblies require accurate tube cutting, welding control, surface treatment and dimensional inspection. They are widely applied in hydraulic cylinders, machinery frames, equipment pipelines and custom industrial hydraulic systems.

Technical Guide

Welded Tube Assemblies Technical Guide

For welded tube assemblies, the bend route, welded adapter orientation and end-to-end distance must be controlled as one assembly. Fixtures should hold tube centerline, port angle and mounting clearance before welding so the finished tube can be installed without forcing the hydraulic line.

Weld heat input can affect tube roundness, coating adhesion and sealing face cleanliness. Drawings should identify weld locations, leak-test requirements, protected threads and any no-weld zones near sleeves, nuts or sealing surfaces.

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Welded tube assemblies commonly use carbon steel or stainless steel tubes with weld-compatible adapters, sleeves and fittings selected for pressure, routing and corrosion exposure. Material choices should consider weldability, tube wall thickness and whether the assembly will be used indoors, outdoors or near corrosive media.

Anti-rust oil, zinc plating, passivation, phosphating or painting can be specified after welding. Post-weld cleaning, spatter removal and protected sealing ends help keep the assembly clean for hydraulic service.
