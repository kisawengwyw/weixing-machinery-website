Welded Tube Assemblies

Welded tube assemblies are customized hydraulic pipe and structural connection components made by combining tubes, adapters, fittings, bosses, brackets or flanges through welding and machining. They are used when standard adapters cannot meet the required routing, strength, shape or installation position. Proper welded tube assemblies require accurate tube cutting, welding control, surface treatment and dimensional inspection. They are widely applied in hydraulic cylinders, machinery frames, equipment pipelines and custom industrial hydraulic systems.

Technical Guide

Common Welded Tube Assembly Types

Common welded tube assemblies include straight welded tube assemblies, bent tube assemblies, tube-to-fitting welded assemblies, welded hydraulic pipe assemblies, welded cylinder tube connections, bracketed tube assemblies and custom welded tube components. Each structure solves a different routing, mounting or connection requirement in equipment where a loose fitting or simple straight pipe is not enough.

Different assembly types are used in hydraulic systems, construction machinery, agricultural equipment, industrial machines and OEM equipment assemblies. For example, bent hydraulic tube assemblies help route oil lines around frames and moving parts, while welded brackets or welded hydraulic fittings can support fixed installation points in compact machinery layouts.

Technical Guide

Tube Bending, Welding and Connection Design

Welded tube assemblies often combine tube bending, cutting, end forming, welding and fitting connection structures in one finished part. Bend radius, tube length, fitting angle, weld position, connection type and installation space should be reviewed together because a small dimensional change can affect how the assembly fits on the machine.

Accurate design helps reduce installation interference and supports stable hydraulic pipeline routing. When the tube path, port orientation and welded connection are controlled from the drawing stage, the finished welded hydraulic pipe assemblies are easier to install and less likely to contact nearby frames, cylinders or moving components.

Technical Guide

Welding Quality and Sealing Requirements

Welding quality is important for hydraulic tube assemblies because welded joints may be exposed to pressure, vibration and repeated equipment movement. Weld penetration, weld bead consistency and joint alignment all affect how well the assembly holds its shape and supports the connected hydraulic line.

Sealing surface protection and post-weld cleaning are also important, especially when welded fittings are close to threaded ports, cone seats or flange faces. Poor welding, spatter, contamination or misalignment may affect sealing, durability and assembly fit, so the weld area and connection area should be considered as one functional interface.

Technical Guide

CNC Machining Requirements for Welded Fittings

Welded tube assemblies may include CNC-machined fittings, threaded connectors, weld adapters, flanges, nuts, sleeves or mounting parts. These CNC machined weld fittings must match both the hydraulic connection requirement and the welding process used on the tube assembly.

Thread accuracy, sealing face quality, weld end dimensions, chamfer control, hex body machining and burr removal are key details for hydraulic welded fittings. Consistent machining helps improve welding fit, sealing reliability and final assembly quality because the welded joint, thread and sealing surface remain aligned with the intended installation position.

Technical Guide

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Common material choices for welded tube assemblies include carbon steel, stainless steel and customer-specified tube or fitting materials, depending on pressure, corrosion resistance and working environment. Material selection should also consider whether the tube, fitting and weld area are compatible with the required forming and welding process.

Surface treatment options may include trivalent zinc plating, zinc nickel plating, black oxide, phosphating, polishing, painting or stainless steel finish where suitable. The material and surface treatment should be selected based on pressure requirement, corrosion resistance, welding process and equipment environment so the finished assembly supports both functional and appearance needs.

Buyer Guide

How to Choose Welded Tube Assemblies

When choosing welded tube assemblies, confirm tube diameter, wall thickness, tube length, bend angle, fitting type, thread standard, weld position, pressure rating, material, surface finish and installation space. These details help define whether the assembly can be installed correctly and whether the hydraulic connection will match the mating port or hose assembly.

For custom welded tube components, buyers should provide drawings, samples, 3D models or assembly requirements whenever possible. Clear information about routing direction, port orientation, mounting points and surface finish expectations helps reduce revision work and supports more stable custom production.
