Hydraulic Valve Bodies

Product Summary

Hydraulic valve bodies are precision-machined components with internal oil passages, valve cavities, ports and sealing surfaces for hydraulic control valves. They require accurate boring, drilling, tapping and surface control so the valve spool, sleeve or cartridge can seal and move correctly.

Hydraulic valve bodies are precision-machined components used to house spools, cartridges, seats, plugs and control passages inside hydraulic valves. They must provide accurate internal geometry, stable sealing surfaces and durable threaded or mounting interfaces. Valve bodies can be produced from carbon steel, alloy steel, stainless steel, cast iron or aluminum depending on pressure and function. They are used in directional control valves, cartridge valves, pressure valves, flow control valves and custom hydraulic control systems.

Technical Guide

Hydraulic Valve Bodies Technical Guide

Valve bodies require accurate valve cavities, port relationships and internal passages so the spool, poppet or cartridge can move and seal correctly. Datum selection should protect the alignment between mounting faces, port threads and functional bores.

Small burrs inside valve cavities or cross holes can affect flow control and sealing. Drawings should identify cavity standards, spool fit areas, sealing lands, port orientation and any pressure or leakage inspection requirements.

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Valve body materials are selected for pressure strength, machinability and corrosion resistance, commonly including carbon steel, alloy steel, aluminum alloy or stainless steel. Oil ports, valve cavities and sealing faces may require different roughness or hardness targets.

Surface treatment should protect the outside without damaging precision cavities, port gauges or sealing lands. Black oxide, zinc plating, anodizing, passivation and local polishing can be specified depending on material and application.
