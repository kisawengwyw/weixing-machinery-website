Hydraulic Valve Bodies

Product Summary

Hydraulic valve bodies are drawing-specific machined housings that provide functional bores, cavities, ports, passages, sealing interfaces and mounting features for a particular valve design. The completed valve function depends on the mating spool, sleeve, poppet, cartridge, seat, spring, plugs and circuit arrangement.

A hydraulic valve body may contain valve bores, cavities, seat locations, cartridge cavities, ports, internal passages, mounting faces, seal grooves and plug locations, but not every body contains spool, sleeve, poppet and cartridge features at the same time. The body provides flow paths and component interfaces; directional, pressure, flow, check or other control functions are determined by the complete valve design and installed components, not by the bare body alone.

Key Features

Hydraulic Valve Bodies Key Features

Specific Valve Geometry

Functional bore geometry, clearances, surface finish and hardness are confirmed with the mating spool, sleeve, poppet, cartridge or seat.

Internal Passage Control

Valve cavity, port and internal passage relationships are controlled from drawing datums so cross holes do not misconnect, interfere with functional bores or create insufficient wall sections.

Plug and Closure Review

Cross-drilled passages that require closure use drawing-specified plugs or sealing structures; plug type, sealing method, installation and inspection are confirmed by the drawing.

Deburring and Cleanliness

Valve bores, cavities, seats, cross holes, blind holes, threaded holes and seal grooves require removal of harmful burrs; visible burr-free areas do not prove hidden intersections are acceptable.

Surface Protection

External finishes must not affect valve bores, cavities, seats, sealing lands, threads, O-ring grooves or mating faces; masking or uncoated areas are drawing-controlled.

Assembly-Level Validation

Bare-body dimensional and passage inspection confirms machining features only; pressure, leakage, flow, actuation and durability are validated on the completed valve assembly when required.

Technical Guide

Hydraulic Valve Bodies Technical Guide

Hydraulic valve bodies require coordinated control of functional bores, cavities, seats, ports, passages, mounting faces and sealing interfaces. The body geometry must match the exact spool, sleeve, poppet, cartridge or seat used in the completed valve.

The bare body provides housing, passages and interfaces. Directional, pressure, flow, check or other control functions depend on the installed moving elements, springs, seals, plugs and complete circuit design.

Dimensional inspection of a bare body does not by itself establish flow performance, internal leakage, pressure rating or valve response. Those properties require assembly-level validation when specified. Cleaning, flushing, particle detection and fixed cleanliness levels are performed only according to the drawing, order or validation plan, with functional bores, cavities, sealing faces and threads protected after cleaning.

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Specific grade, heat treatment and blank form are confirmed by the drawing. Cast iron or ductile iron is supplied only when the drawing, blank source and project requirements are defined.

Grinding, lapping, polishing, heat treatment or coating is used only when specified. Functional bores may need to remain uncoated, masked, ground, hardened or locally treated according to the drawing.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hydraulic valve body?
A hydraulic valve body is a drawing-specific machined housing that provides functional bores, cavities, ports, passages, sealing interfaces and mounting features for a particular valve design.

How is it different from a hydraulic manifold block?
A valve body directly houses or interfaces with moving or sealing valve elements such as spool bores, sleeve fits, poppet seats or cartridge cavities. A manifold block primarily integrates passages and mounting interfaces for multiple components and does not necessarily contain a direct spool bore or valve seat.

Does the bare valve body control pressure or flow?
No. The bare body provides housing, passages and interfaces. Directional, pressure, flow, check or other control functions depend on installed moving elements, springs, seals, plugs and the complete circuit design.

What valve-body designs can be manufactured?
Drawing-specific direct spool-bore, sleeve-type, cartridge-cavity and poppet or seat valve bodies can be reviewed and machined when the mating component data and acceptance criteria are provided.

How is a valve body manufactured?
Manufacturing may include CNC milling, drilling, tapping, precision boring or reaming, valve-bore or cartridge-cavity machining, seat and sealing-interface machining, internal-passage machining, deburring, cleaning and drawing-specified secondary processing.

Is welding a standard valve-body manufacturing process?
No. Welding is not a standard process for internal functional bores and passages. Any welded attachment is a separate project-specific feature and requires independent review.

Which materials are available?
Carbon steel, stainless steel and aluminum alloy are commonly reviewed. Alloy steel, cast iron, ductile iron or other materials are subject to drawing, pressure, media, wear, fatigue, casting condition and application review.

How are spool, sleeve, cartridge or poppet fits confirmed?
Functional bore geometry, clearances, surface finish and hardness are confirmed with the mating spool, sleeve, poppet, cartridge or seat. No single clearance or roughness requirement applies to every valve-body design.

How is working pressure confirmed?
Pressure suitability is confirmed according to material and grade, minimum wall sections, bore and cavity geometry, internal passages, ports, plugs, sealing interfaces, mounted components, media, temperature, pressure cycle and complete valve validation.

Can a bare body establish internal leakage or flow performance?
No. Flow capacity, pressure control, directional response, internal leakage, hysteresis and operating force depend on the completed valve assembly and cannot be established from the bare valve body alone.

How are burrs and internal cleanliness controlled?
Valve bores, cavities, seats, cross holes, blind holes, threaded holes and seal grooves require removal of harmful burrs. Cleaning, flushing, particle checks and cleanliness levels are performed only when required by the drawing, order or validation plan.

Are all valve bodies pressure or function tested?
No. Bare-body dimensional and passage inspection confirms machining features only. Pressure, leakage, flow, actuation and durability performance is validated on the applicable completed valve assembly when required.

What surface treatments are available?
Material-compatible external finishes include trivalent zinc plating, zinc-nickel coating, black oxide or phosphating for suitable steel bodies, anodizing or conversion coating for suitable aluminum bodies, and passivation or natural finish for stainless steel.

How are tolerances and hardness confirmed?
Functional bores, cavities, seats, sealing lands, port positions, passage intersections, mounting faces, thread depths, surface finish and hardness are controlled according to the drawing, mating components, valve function and agreed inspection plan.

What information is required for quotation?
Please provide the latest valve assembly drawing, hydraulic schematic, 2D drawings, 3D models, mating spool, sleeve, cartridge, poppet, seat, spring and plug data, material and finish requirements, test requirements, quantity and samples when available.
