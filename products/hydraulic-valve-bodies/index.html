Hydraulic Valve Bodies

Hydraulic valve bodies are precision-machined components used to house spools, cartridges, seats, plugs and control passages inside hydraulic valves. They must provide accurate internal geometry, stable sealing surfaces and durable threaded or mounting interfaces. Valve bodies can be produced from carbon steel, alloy steel, stainless steel, cast iron or aluminum depending on pressure and function. They are used in directional control valves, cartridge valves, pressure valves, flow control valves and custom hydraulic control systems.

Technical Guide

Common Hydraulic Valve Body Types

Common hydraulic valve bodies include directional control valve bodies, cartridge valve bodies, proportional valve bodies, pressure control valve bodies, flow control valve bodies, custom machined valve bodies and multi-port hydraulic valve housings. Each design supports a different control function, oil route arrangement and installation method.

Directional and hydraulic control valve bodies are often used to guide actuator movement, while cartridge valve bodies can integrate compact logic functions inside a manifold or block. Different hydraulic valve housings are selected for hydraulic power units, mobile machinery, industrial equipment, construction equipment and OEM hydraulic systems according to circuit layout and service conditions.

Technical Guide

Valve Cavities, Ports and Flow Passages

Hydraulic valve bodies usually include precision valve cavities, threaded ports, internal oil passages, sealing surfaces and mounting holes. Cavity size, spool bore accuracy, port layout, flow path design and sealing surface control are important for stable valve operation and predictable hydraulic response.

When internal passages are too restrictive, misaligned or poorly deburred, pressure control, flow stability and leakage performance may be affected. For custom valve bodies, drawings should clearly define cross-drilled passages, plug positions, port standards and sealing areas so the machined part matches the intended hydraulic circuit.

Technical Guide

CNC Machining Requirements for Valve Bodies

Hydraulic valve bodies require accurate CNC milling, drilling, boring, reaming, tapping and deburring. Key machining controls include bore concentricity, valve cavity depth, thread accuracy, sealing surface flatness, edge deburring and dimensional consistency across repeated parts.

These controls are especially important for CNC machined valve bodies that work with valve spools, pistons, plugs, springs or cartridge valve assemblies. Accurate machining helps the moving and sealing components fit correctly and reduces the risk of assembly interference, unstable movement or unwanted leakage paths.

Technical Guide

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Common material choices for hydraulic valve bodies include carbon steel, alloy steel, ductile iron, aluminum and stainless steel. The selection depends on pressure rating, weight requirement, corrosion resistance, machining characteristics and the equipment environment.

Surface finish options may include black oxide, zinc plating, zinc nickel plating, anodizing, phosphating, polishing or customer-specified surface treatments where suitable. Material and surface finish should be selected based on working pressure, hydraulic fluid compatibility, corrosion resistance requirements and the service environment of the equipment.

Technical Guide

How to Choose Hydraulic Valve Bodies

When selecting hydraulic valve bodies, buyers should check the hydraulic schematic, valve function, port size, thread standard, spool or cartridge cavity type, pressure rating, material, surface finish, mounting hole layout, sealing method and available installation space. These details determine whether the valve body can be assembled, connected and serviced correctly.

For custom valve bodies, it is helpful to provide engineering drawings, samples, valve assembly requirements or hydraulic circuit information before quotation. Clear information about port orientation, cavity standards and sealing requirements can reduce redesign work and help align the CNC machined valve bodies with the final hydraulic system.
