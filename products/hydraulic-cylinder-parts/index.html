Product Summary

Hydraulic cylinder parts include pistons, glands, end caps, rod ends, clevis ends, ports and welded ends manufactured for sealing, guidance and load transfer. Machining focuses on seal grooves, bore fits, threads, pin holes and welded structures according to cylinder drawings.

Hydraulic cylinder parts include pistons, glands, rod ends, clevises, bushings, welding heads, ports and other components used in hydraulic cylinders. These parts must withstand pressure, sliding movement, mechanical load and long-term wear. Accurate machining, suitable material selection and proper surface treatment are important for cylinder sealing, guiding and connection performance. Hydraulic cylinder parts are used in construction machinery, lifting equipment, agricultural machines, industrial presses and custom hydraulic actuator systems.

Selection Advice

Hydraulic Cylinder Parts Selection Advice

1. Confirm drawing requirements. Provide key dimensions, tolerances, threads, holes, grooves and surface roughness.

2. Confirm material. Select material according to pressure, weldability, corrosion resistance and cost.

3. Confirm surface finish. Specify salt-spray, color, coating or anti-rust packaging requirements.

4. Confirm inspection focus. Mark sealing faces, fitted bores, threads and critical dimensions.

Technical Guide

Hydraulic Cylinder Parts Technical Guide

Cylinder parts require controlled bores, outside diameters, seal grooves and rod-end features because each surface affects sealing, sliding and assembly life. Machining datums should follow the functional fit between piston, rod, end cap and gland components.

Seal groove width, depth, corner radius and surface roughness are critical. Drawings should clearly mark groove standards, pin-hole positions, thread engagement, chrome-contact surfaces and any areas requiring polishing or coating.

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Pistons, rod ends, end caps and glands are usually produced from carbon steel, alloy steel, stainless steel or bronze-related materials according to strength, wear and corrosion needs. Material selection should match seal type, cylinder pressure and mating rod or bore condition.

Surface treatments may include black oxide, zinc plating, phosphating, hard chrome on related rod surfaces, polishing or anti-rust protection. Seal grooves and sliding surfaces should be protected from coating buildup or scratches.
