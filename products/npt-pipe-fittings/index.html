NPT Pipe Fittings

Product Summary

NPT pipe fittings use American tapered pipe threads for compact threaded ports, pipe adapters, plugs and instrumentation connections. Correct sealant selection, thread engagement and port verification are important because NPT sealing differs from flare or face-seal systems. Wei Xing Machinery can machine custom NPT fittings with specified lengths, hex sizes and conversion ends.

NPT pipe fittings are threaded hydraulic and industrial pipe connectors using National Pipe Taper thread geometry. They are often selected for compact threaded ports, pipe adapters, hose tail connections and instrumentation interfaces. The tapered thread creates sealing contact during tightening and is commonly used together with suitable sealant. NPT pipe fittings are available in straight, elbow, tee, plug and conversion styles for hydraulic machinery, fluid transfer lines, maintenance systems and custom equipment assemblies.

Selection Advice

How to Choose NPT Pipe Fittings

1. Confirm the thread is NPT. Use the drawing, gauge or mating port to confirm the thread system.

2. Confirm nominal size and gender. Check the nominal size and male/female connection before ordering.

3. Check the port condition. Review port material, wall thickness and allowable engagement.

4. Select fitting configuration and opposite-end connection. Choose the body shape and the required opposite-end interface.

5. Select body material and surface treatment. Confirm options according to the application and order requirements.

6. Select a compatible pipe-thread sealant. Confirm compatibility with the media and temperature.

7. Confirm system requirements. Avoid excessive tightening during assembly.

8. Provide drawings or samples for custom production. Send drawings, samples or assembly requirements when custom dimensions are needed.

Technical Guide

Technical Guide

NPT connections rely on controlled engagement between male and female tapered pipe threads. A compatible pipe-thread sealant is normally selected to help seal the helical leakage path and reduce galling risk. Confirm the thread standard, port material, engagement, media, temperature and system requirements before assembly.

Use only a compatible pipe-thread sealant approved for the fluid, temperature and system. Apply it to the appropriate male NPT thread and prevent excess material from entering the fluid passage. Pipe-thread sealant is not used as the primary seal on JIC flare faces or ORFS face-seal connections.

Do not over-tighten NPT fittings. Excessive taper-thread wedging can damage thin-wall ports, crack cast components or distort manifold ports. Do not mix NPT with BSPT merely because both are tapered pipe threads; confirm with the correct gauge or drawing.

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Materials and Surface Finish Options

Carbon steel is commonly considered for general hydraulic machinery, while stainless steel can be evaluated for corrosion-sensitive environments. FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an NPT pipe fitting?
An NPT pipe fitting is a threaded connection component using tapered pipe threads for pipe ports, gauges, manifolds, auxiliary circuits and adapters.

What standard is used?
NPT tapered pipe threads can be manufactured according to ASME B1.20.1 when specified by the drawing and order.

How does an NPT connection seal?
NPT connections rely on controlled taper-thread engagement with a compatible pipe-thread sealant selected for the fluid, temperature, pressure, port material and system.

Is thread sealant required?
A compatible pipe-thread sealant is normally selected for the appropriate male NPT thread; prevent excess material from entering the fluid passage.

Can NPT and BSPT be connected together?
NPT and BSPT are different thread systems and should not be treated as interchangeable. Confirm thread form, pitch, taper and mating port before assembly.

Can NPT sealant be used on JIC or ORFS connections?
No. Pipe-thread sealant is not used as the primary seal on JIC flare faces or ORFS face-seal connections.

What materials are available?
Carbon steel and stainless steel are available; brass is available only after media, pressure and project review.

What surface treatments are available?
Available processes may include trivalent zinc plating, zinc-nickel coating, black oxide, phosphating or natural stainless finish, subject to material and order requirements.

How is working pressure confirmed?
Working requirements are confirmed according to thread size, body material, port condition, engagement length, fitting configuration and system requirements.

Can custom NPT fittings be manufactured by drawing?
Yes. Wei Xing Machinery can manufacture custom NPT fittings according to drawings, samples, material, surface treatment and assembly requirements.
