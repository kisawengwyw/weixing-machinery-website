BSP vs NPT Threads: Differences, Sealing and Identification

A practical guide to BSPP, BSPT and NPT thread identification and selection.

Prepared by: Wei Xing Machinery Technical Team
2026-07-13

Technical content for hydraulic component selection and sourcing reference.

Introduction

BSP and NPT are pipe thread families used in hydraulic and industrial connections. They are not interchangeable simply because nominal sizes appear similar; selection must be verified by measurement, drawing and mating port specification.

What Is BSP?

BSP is a British Standard Pipe thread family. It includes BSPP parallel thread and BSPT tapered thread, both based on a 55-degree Whitworth thread form.

BSPP vs BSPT

BSPP is parallel and may seal through a bonded washer, O-ring, cone seat or another specified sealing interface. BSPT is tapered and normally relies on tapered thread engagement with a suitable sealing method where required.

What Is NPT?

NPT is a tapered pipe thread with a 60-degree thread form. It commonly seals through tapered thread interference with suitable thread sealant where required.

Thread Angle and Profile

The difference between 55-degree BSP and 60-degree NPT thread forms is one reason similar nominal sizes should not be treated as compatible.

Parallel vs Tapered Threads

Confirm whether the thread is parallel or tapered by measurement rather than appearance alone.

Sealing Methods

Identify the specified sealing interface, washer, O-ring, cone seat or sealant requirements before ordering.

How to Identify BSP and NPT

Use a thread gauge, outside diameter, pitch, taper check, mating port specification and drawing or equipment documentation. Appearance alone is not enough.

Why They Should Not Be Interchanged

Different thread forms and sealing methods can cause poor fit or sealing problems if mismatched.

Information Needed for Selection

  • Thread family and size
  • Outside diameter and pitch
  • Taper check
  • Mating port specification
  • Drawing or equipment documentation

FAQ

Are BSP and NPT threads interchangeable?

No. BSP and NPT should not be interchanged solely because nominal sizes appear similar; the thread angle, profile and sealing method differ.

What is the difference between BSPP and BSPT?

BSPP is a parallel thread, while BSPT is a tapered thread. Both use a 55-degree Whitworth thread form.

Do BSPP threads seal on the thread?

BSPP may seal through a bonded washer, O-ring, cone seat or another specified sealing interface rather than relying only on the parallel thread.

What thread angle does NPT use?

NPT uses a 60-degree thread form.

How can I measure an unknown thread?

Use a thread gauge, outside diameter measurement, pitch measurement, taper check and the mating port specification or drawing.
